## CASE STUDY 1: PCI DSS Compliance Program — Saudi National Bank

**Client:** Saudi National Bank (SNB)  
**Industry:** Banking & Financial Services  
**Duration:** Multi-year recurring engagement  
**Location:** Saudi Arabia  
**Team:** Lead Consultant  

### Business Challenge
SNB, one of the largest banks in the Middle East, needed to maintain PCI DSS compliance while undergoing significant organizational changes, including mergers, acquisitions, and the integration of new systems. Non-compliance posed both regulatory and financial risks.

### Objectives
- Maintain PCI DSS compliance across all bank divisions
- Reduce compliance gaps and deficiencies
- Streamline evidence collection and reporting
- Ensure audit readiness at all times

### Approach
We implemented a comprehensive PCI DSS program covering all 12 requirements and approximately 280 controls. The program included:

1. A thorough gap assessment of all requirements
2. Control mapping and evidence identification
3. Weekly testing and documentation of controls
4. Coordination with internal teams to address gaps
5. On-going checking with operative staff

### Methodology
GRCA framework applied to PCI DSS v3.2.1/v4.0 controls. Built a compliance function that operated around closure of non-compliant items.

### Deliverables
- Compliance program charter
- Gap assessment report
- Control matrix and evidence list
- Policy/process update recommendations
- Monthly management reporting
- Audit readiness letter

### Business Value
The bank achieved sustained compliance without any major interruptions to operations or business delivery. Teams built an internal capability that reduced dependency on external resources.

### Results
- 60% reduction in compliance gaps
- Successful external audit readiness
- Improved visibility for senior management
- Team autonomy in evidence collection and review

### Lessons Learned
- Early internal stakeholder identification is critical
- Continuous training reduces resistance and increases engagement
- Compliance is a constant journey, not a one-off project

## CASE STUDY 2: Enterprise Security Architecture Review — Diriyah Project

**Client:** Confidential (Government-backed project)  
**Industry:** Real Estate, Infrastructure  
**Location:** Saudi Arabia  

### Business Challenge
A national-level project required a comprehensive security architecture review prior to expanding its digital footprint. The environment consisted of multiple systems, cloud components, and OT/IT converged networks.

### Objectives
- Identify potential security vulnerabilities
- Evaluate current architecture against best practices
- Provide prioritized, actionable recommendations
- Align improvements with a long-term roadmap

### Approach
We conducted a thorough architecture discovery and analysis, involving:

- Document review (architecture diagrams, data flow, current controls)
- Stakeholder interviews
- Network segmentation analysis
- Cloud security posture evaluation
- Application security testing (static/dynamic)
- IAM and logging assessment assessment

### Methodology
NIST SP 800-53 Rev.5 and CIS Controls v8. Additionally, we applied STRIDE and DREAD for threat modeling.

### Deliverables
- Architecture diagram with security gaps
- Detailed findings report
- Risk-scored recommendation list
- Remediation roadmap

### Business Value
The organization obtained a clear picture of its security posture, which enabled it to plan infrastructure improvements with confidence.

### Results
- 30+ findings identified including 12 critical
- All findings prioritised and addressed
- Enhanced security program for all teams
- Improved incident response preparations

### Lessons Learned
- Architecture review should be a recurring activity
- Threat modeling early in the project lifecycle prevents issues
- Collaboration between engineering and security is essential

## CASE STUDY 3: SOC 2 Readiness — NVITS (USA)

**Client:** NVITS (name anonymized)  
**Industry:** IT Services & Consulting  
**Location:** United States  

### Business Challenge
A US-based IT services company aimed to achieve SOC 2 Type I certification to meet client demands and increase market share, but had no prior experience with SOC 2 or compliance process.

### Objectives
- Understand Trust Service Criteria requirements
- Identify current state vs. required controls
- Build a sustainable evidence gathering process
- Educate team on SOC 2 expectations

### Approach
We executed a five-phase SOC 2 readiness assessment:

1. Scoping and planning
2. Current state review of controls
3. Framework mapping to Trust Service Criteria
4. Gap analysis for each criterion
5. Evidence gap identification and recommendations

### Methodology
SOC 2 TSC and AICPA guidance for each trust principle.

### Deliverables
- Readiness assessment report
- Gap analysis against TSC
- Documented control mapping
- Evidence collection templates and guidelines
- Remediation suggestions with priorities

### Business Value
NVITS gained a clear roadmap to SOC 2 compliance. Senior management could make informed decisions about resource allocation.

### Results
- 100% of Trust Service Categories assessed
- Actionable gap analysis
- Standard operating procedures documented
- Team learned SOC 2 language and concepts

### Lessons Learned
- Starting with an internal assessment is less intimidating than jumping into a full audit.
- Early engagement of operational teams is crucial.

## CASE STUDY 4: SOC 2 Compliance — Parent.app

**Client:** Parent.app (name anonymized)  
**Industry:** Technology / Mobile Apps  
**Location:** United States  

### Business Challenge
A fast-moving startup needed to implement SOC 2 compliance to satisfy enterprise client requirements while maintaining a high-velocity development cycle.

### Objectives
- Achieve SOC 2 compliance
- Integrate controls into existing workflows without slowing down
- Educate team on security practices

### Approach
Developed a streamlined SOC 2 compliance program:

- TSC mapping tailored to start-up constraints
- Policy writing and staff training
- Automated evidence collection using existing tools
- Regular progress and evaluation cadences

### Methodology
SOC 2 TSC, minimal but effective control set.

### Deliverables
- SOC 2 evidence package
- Policy documents for privacy, security, and data handling
- Testing results for key controls

### Business Value
The company achieved compliance with minimal overhead, enabling it to serve enterprise customers and drive growth.

### Results
- Compliance program ready for external audit
- Staff awareness improved across the organization
- Reduced time and cost of evidence collection

### Lessons Learned
- A pragmatic approach focusing on the highest risk areas works best for startups.
- Automating evidence collection is a high-leverage activity.

## CASE STUDY 5: Healthcare SaaS Security Review

**Client:** Healthcare Physiotherapy Platform (name anonymized)  
**Industry:** Healthcare  
**Location:** [Country not disclosed]

### Business Challenge
A physiotherapy services platform needed a security review prior to starting HIPAA and SOC 2 compliance initiatives. The platform handled sensitive patient information and needed to ensure a solid security foundation.

### Objective
- Baseline security assessment
- Identify and prioritize vulnerabilities
- Create a compliance roadmap for HIPAA/SOC 2

### Approach
Two-week deep-dive involving:

- Review of code, infrastructure, and data management
- Network architecture and application logic analysis
- Access controls and authentication analysis
- Data protection and privacy practices
- Logging, monitoring, and incident handling review

### Methodology
NIST CSF, OWASP Top 10, and CIS benchmarks.

### Deliverables
- Report with 20+ findings and severity ranking
- Prioritized remediation plan
- Security improvement roadmap

### Business Value
Early discovery of issues allowed cheaper and faster remediation. Credible risk assessment for investors.

### Results
- All critical and high-risk issues addressed
- Compliance roadmap defined for HIPAA/SOC 2
- Improved developer security awareness

### Lessons Learned
- Early security engagement reduces the cost of fixing issues later.
- Many issues are common across organizations.

## CASE STUDY 6: SAMA CSF Implementation

**Client:** Insurance company (anonymized)  
**Industry:** Insurance  
**Location:** Saudi Arabia  

### Business Challenge
A growing insurance firm in Saudi Arabia needed to comply with the Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) cybersecurity framework to meet regulatory requirements and license conditions.

### Objectives
- Assess current state against SAMA CSF
- Implement controls to fill gaps
- Remap the organization to a security-compliant mode
- Achieve regulatory approval

### Approach
1. Gap analysis against all SAMA CSF domains
2. Implementation planning and prioritization
3. Control implementation in collaboration with IT and business
4. Testing and validation of controls
5. Documentation and training on new processes

### Methodology
SAMA CSF framework, ISO 27001 controls as foundation for many requirements.

### Deliverables
- SAMA gap assessment report
- Implementation roadmap
- Updated policies and procedures
- Training and workshop sessions
- Evidence repository for each domain

### Business Value
The firm avoided regulatory penalties, build trust with stakeholders, and improved risk management maturity.

### Results
- Achieved compliance with SAMA CSF
- Passed regulatory review
- Improved risk management maturity
- Strengthened internal controls

### Lessons Learned
- A phased approach to implementation makes the process manageable.
- Involving staff in test scenarios reduces resistance and increases effectiveness.
